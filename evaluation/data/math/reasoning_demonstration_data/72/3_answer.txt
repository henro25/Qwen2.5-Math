first, we square both sides of the given equation:

\[
\left( m + \frac{1}{m} \right)^{2} = 8^{2}
\]

expanding the left side:

\[
m^{2} + 2 \cdot m \cdot \frac{1}{m} + \frac{1}{m^{2}} = 64
\]

simplifying the middle term:

\[
m^{2} + 2 + \frac{1}{m^{2}} = 64
\]

subtracting 2 from both sides:

\[
m^{2} + \frac{1}{m^{2}} = 62
\]

adding 4 to both sides:

\[
m^{2} + \frac{1}{m^{2}} + 4 = 62 + 4 = 66
\]

thus, the value of \( m^{2} + \frac{1}{m^{2}} + 4 \) is \(\boxed{66}\).
