Answer: To find the average percent score for the 100 students, we need to calculate the total sum of all the percent scores and then divide by the total number of students.

given the data:

\[
\begin{array}{|c|c|}
\hline
\% \text{ score} & \text{number of students} \\
\hline
100 & 7 \\
\hline
90 & 18 \\
\hline
80 & 35 \\
\hline
70 & 25 \\
\hline
60 & 10 \\
\hline
50 & 3 \\
\hline
40 & 2 \\
\hline
\end{array}
\]

we calculate the total sum of all scores:

\[
\begin{align*}
100 \times 7 &= 700 \\
90 \times 18 &= 1620 \\
80 \times 35 &= 2800 \\
70 \times 25 &= 1750 \\
60 \times 10 &= 600 \\
50 \times 3 &= 150 \\
40 \times 2 &= 80 \\
\end{align*}
\]

adding these products together:

\[
700 + 1620 + 2800 + 1750 + 600 + 150 + 80 = 7700
\]

the average percent score is then:

\[
\bar{p} = \frac{7700}{100} = 77
\]

therefore, the average percent score for these 100 students is \boxed{77}.
