Answer: First, we solve the quadratic equation \( x^2 = x + 1 \) by rewriting it in standard form:

\[ x^2 - x - 1 = 0 \]

using the quadratic formula \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \) with \( a = 1 \), \( b = -1 \), and \( c = -1 \):

\[ x = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{1 + 4}}{2} = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{5}}{2} \]

thus, the solutions are:

\[ \phi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \]
\[ \varphi = \frac{1 - \sqrt{5}}{2} \]

next, we find \( (\phi - \varphi)^2 \):

\[ \phi - \varphi = \left( \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \right) - \left( \frac{1 - \sqrt{5}}{2} \right) = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{2} = \sqrt{5} \]

therefore:

\[ (\phi - \varphi)^2 = (\sqrt{5})^2 = 5 \]

alternatively, using vieta's formulas for the sum and product of the roots:

\[ \phi + \varphi = 1 \]
\[ \phi \varphi = -1 \]

we use the identity:

\[ (\phi - \varphi)^2 = (\phi + \varphi)^2 - 4\phi\varphi \]

substituting the values:

\[ (\phi - \varphi)^2 = 1^2 - 4(-1) = 1 + 4 = 5 \]

thus, the value of \( (\phi - \varphi)^2 \) is:

\[
\boxed{5}
