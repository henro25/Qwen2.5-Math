Answer: To solve for \( f(g(x)) - g(f(x)) \), we are given the functions:

\[
f(x) = 6x - 9 \quad \text{and} \quad g(x) = \frac{x}{3} + 2
\]

### Step 1: Find \( f(g(x)) \)
First, substitute \( g(x) \) into \( f(x) \):

\[
f(g(x)) = f\left( \frac{x}{3} + 2 \right) = 6\left( \frac{x}{3} + 2 \right) - 9
\]

Simplify:

\[
6 \cdot \frac{x}{3} + 6 \cdot 2 - 9 = 2x + 12 - 9 = 2x + 3
\]

### Step 2: Find \( g(f(x)) \)
Next, substitute \( f(x) \) into \( g(x) \):

\[
g(f(x)) = g(6x - 9) = \frac{6x - 9}{3} + 2
\]

Simplify:

\[
\frac{6x}{3} - \frac{9}{3} + 2 = 2x - 3 + 2 = 2x - 1
\]

### Step 3: Calculate \( f(g(x)) - g(f(x)) \)
Now, subtract \( g(f(x)) \) from \( f(g(x)) \):

\[
f(g(x)) - g(f(x)) = (2x + 3) - (2x - 1)
\]

Simplify:

\[
2x + 3 - 2x + 1 = 4
\]

Thus, the final answer is:

\[
\boxed{4}
\]
