Answer: To simplify $(\sqrt{5})^4$, we can use exponent rules.

1. **Express the square root as an exponent:**
   \[
   \sqrt{5} = 5^{1/2}
   \]

2. **Apply the power rule for exponents:**
   \[
   (5^{1/2})^4 = 5^{(1/2) \times 4} = 5^2
   \]

3. **Simplify the exponentiation:**
   \[
   5^2 = 25
   \]

Thus, $(\sqrt{5})^4$ simplifies to $25$.
