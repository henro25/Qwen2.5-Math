Answer: 1. **Understand the problem:**  
   - Angles \( A \) and \( B \) are supplementary, meaning \( A + B = 180^\circ \).
   - Angle \( A \) is 8 times angle \( B \), so \( A = 8B \).

2. **Set up the equation:**  
   - Since \( A + B = 180^\circ \), substitute \( A \) with \( 8B \):
     \[
     8B + B = 180^\circ
     \]
   - Combine like terms:
     \[
     9B = 180^\circ
     \]

3. **Solve for \( B \):**  
   - Divide both sides by 9:
     \[
     B = \frac{180^\circ}{9} = 20^\circ
     \]

4. **Find \( A \):**  
   - Since \( A = 8B \), substitute \( B = 20^\circ \):
     \[
     A = 8 \times 20^\circ = 160^\circ
     \]

5. **Verify the solution:**  
   - Check that \( A + B = 180^\circ \):
     \[
     160^\circ + 20^\circ = 180^\circ
     \]
   - The angles add up correctly.

**Answer:** The measure of angle \( A \) is \( 160^\circ \).
