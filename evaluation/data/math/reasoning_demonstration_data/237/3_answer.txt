Answer: To find the cross product of the vectors \(\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 2 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}\), we use the determinant method:

\[
\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \begin{vmatrix}
\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\
5 & 2 & -6 \\
1 & 1 & 3 \\
\end{vmatrix}
\]

expanding this determinant, we get:

\[
\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = \mathbf{i}(2 \cdot 3 - (-6) \cdot 1) - \mathbf{j}(5 \cdot 3 - (-6) \cdot 1) + \mathbf{k}(5 \cdot 1 - 2 \cdot 1)
\]

calculating each component:

\[
\mathbf{i} \text{ component: } 2 \cdot 3 - (-6) \cdot 1 = 6 + 6 = 12
\]
\[
\mathbf{j} \text{ component: } 5 \cdot 3 - (-6) \cdot 1 = 15 + 6 = 21 \quad (\text{with a negative sign, it becomes } -21)
\]
\[
\mathbf{k} \text{ component: } 5 \cdot 1 - 2 \cdot 1 = 5 - 2 = 3
\]

thus, the cross product is:

\[
\mathbf{a} \times \mathbf{b} = 12\mathbf{i} - 21\mathbf{j} + 3\mathbf{k}
\]

in vector form, this is:

\[
\boxed{\begin{pmatrix} 12 \\ -21 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}}
