Answer: Given the equations:

1. \( x + y = \frac{7}{12} \)
2. \( x - y = \frac{1}{12} \)

we need to find \( x^2 - y^2 \). using the difference of squares formula:

\[ x^2 - y^2 = (x + y)(x - y) \]

substituting the given values:

\[ x^2 - y^2 = \left( \frac{7}{12} \right) \left( \frac{1}{12} \right) = \frac{7 \times 1}{12 \times 12} = \frac{7}{144} \]

to verify, we solve for \( x \) and \( y \) individually. adding the two equations:

\[ (x + y) + (x - y) = \frac{7}{12} + \frac{1}{12} \]
\[ 2x = \frac{8}{12} \]
\[ x = \frac{8}{24} = \frac{1}{3} \]

substituting \( x = \frac{1}{3} \) back into the first equation:

\[ \frac{1}{3} + y = \frac{7}{12} \]
\[ y = \frac{7}{12} - \frac{1}{3} \]
\[ y = \frac{7}{12} - \frac{4}{12} = \frac{3}{12} = \frac{1}{4} \]

now, calculating \( x^2 - y^2 \):

\[ x^2 = \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^2 = \frac{1}{9} \]
\[ y^2 = \left( \frac{1}{4} \right)^2 = \frac{1}{16} \]
\[ x^2 - y^2 = \frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{16} \]

finding a common denominator:

\[ \frac{1}{9} = \frac{16}{144} \]
\[ \frac{1}{16} = \frac{9}{144} \]
\[ x^2 - y^2 = \frac{16}{144} - \frac{9}{144} = \frac{7}{144} \]

thus, the value of \( x^2 - y^2 \) is:

\[
\boxed{\frac{7}{144}}
\]
