first, we determine the lengths of the sides for each triangle using the pythagorean theorem.

for triangle \(bad\):
\[
ab^2 + ad^2 = bd^2 \implies 12^2 + ad^2 = 15^2 \implies 144 + ad^2 = 225 \implies ad^2 = 81 \implies ad = 9 \text{ units}
\]

for triangle \(bdc\):
\[
bd^2 + dc^2 = bc^2 \implies 15^2 + dc^2 = 17^2 \implies 225 + dc^2 = 289 \implies dc^2 = 64 \implies dc = 8 \text{ units}
\]

next, we calculate the areas of the two right triangles.

area of triangle \(bad\):
\[
\text{area}_{bad} = \frac{1}{2} \times ab \times ad = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 9 = 54 \text{ square units}
\]

area of triangle \(bdc\):
\[
\text{area}_{bdc} = \frac{1}{2} \times bd \times dc = \frac{1}{2} \times 15 \times 8 = 60 \text{ square units}
\]

finally, the area of quadrilateral \(abcd\) is the sum of the areas of triangles \(bad\) and \(bdc\):
\[
\text{area}_{abcd} = \text{area}_{bad} + \text{area}_{bdc} = 54 + 60 = 114 \text{ square units}
\]
