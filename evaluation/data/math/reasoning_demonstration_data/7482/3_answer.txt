Answer: To simplify \(\dfrac{3+4i}{1+2i}\) and express it in the form \(a + bi\), we multiply the numerator and the denominator by the conjugate of the denominator:

\[
\dfrac{3 + 4i}{1 + 2i} \times \dfrac{1 - 2i}{1 - 2i}
\]

the denominator becomes:

\[
(1 + 2i)(1 - 2i) = 1 - (2i)^2 = 1 - 4(-1) = 1 + 4 = 5
\]

the numerator becomes:

\[
(3 + 4i)(1 - 2i) = 3 \cdot 1 + 3 \cdot (-2i) + 4i \cdot 1 + 4i \cdot (-2i) = 3 - 6i + 4i - 8i^2
\]

since \(i^2 = -1\):

\[
3 - 6i + 4i - 8(-1) = 3 - 2i + 8 = 11 - 2i
\]

thus, the fraction simplifies to:

\[
\dfrac{11 - 2i}{5} = \dfrac{11}{5} - \dfrac{2i}{5}
\]

therefore, the final answer is:

\[
\boxed{\dfrac{11}{5} - \dfrac{2}{5}i}
\]
