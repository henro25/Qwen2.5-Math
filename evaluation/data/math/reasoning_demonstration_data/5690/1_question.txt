Problem: A polynomial of degree four with leading coefficient 1 and integer coefficients has two real zeros, both of which are integers. Which of the following can also be a zero of the polynomial?

(A) $\frac{1 + i \sqrt{11}}{2}$

(B) $\frac{1 + i}{2}$

(C) $\frac{1}{2} + i$

(D) $1 + \frac{i}{2}$

(E) $\frac{1 + i \sqrt{13}}{2}$
