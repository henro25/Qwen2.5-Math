Answer: Given the equations:

1. \( xyz = 1 \)
2. \( x + \frac{1}{z} = 5 \)
3. \( y + \frac{1}{x} = 29 \)

we need to find \( z + \frac{1}{y} \).

first, solve for \( z \) from the first equation:
\[ z = \frac{1}{xy} \]

substitute \( z \) into the second equation:
\[ x + \frac{1}{\frac{1}{xy}} = 5 \]
\[ x + xy = 5 \]
\[ x(1 + y) = 5 \]
\[ x = \frac{5}{1 + y} \]

substitute \( x \) into the third equation:
\[ y + \frac{1}{\frac{5}{1 + y}} = 29 \]
\[ y + \frac{1 + y}{5} = 29 \]
\[ 5y + 1 + y = 145 \]
\[ 6y + 1 = 145 \]
\[ 6y = 144 \]
\[ y = 24 \]

now, find \( x \):
\[ x = \frac{5}{1 + 24} = \frac{5}{25} = \frac{1}{5} \]

next, find \( z \):
\[ z = \frac{1}{xy} = \frac{1}{\left( \frac{1}{5} \right) \times 24} = \frac{1}{\frac{24}{5}} = \frac{5}{24} \]

finally, find \( z + \frac{1}{y} \):
\[ z + \frac{1}{y} = \frac{5}{24} + \frac{1}{24} = \frac{6}{24} = \frac{1}{4} \]

thus, the answer is:
\[
\boxed{\frac{1}{4}}
