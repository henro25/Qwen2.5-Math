Answer: To evaluate the product \((n-1) \cdot n \cdot (n+1) \cdot (n+2) \cdot (n+3)\) where \(n = 2\), we start by substituting \(n = 2\) into the expression:

\[
(2-1) \cdot 2 \cdot (2+1) \cdot (2+2) \cdot (2+3)
\]

simplifying each term inside the parentheses:

\[
1 \cdot 2 \cdot 3 \cdot 4 \cdot 5
\]

next, we multiply these numbers step by step:

\[
1 \cdot 2 = 2
\]
\[
2 \cdot 3 = 6
\]
\[
6 \cdot 4 = 24
\]
\[
24 \cdot 5 = 120
\]

thus, the product is:

\[
\boxed{120}
\]
