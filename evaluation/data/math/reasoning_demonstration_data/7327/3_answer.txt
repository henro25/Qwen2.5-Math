Answer: To solve the problem:

1. **Identify the sides of the triangle:**
   - The longest side (hypotenuse) is 5 meters.
   - The shortest side (one leg) is 3 meters.

2. **Use the Pythagorean theorem to find the other leg:**
   \[
   a^2 + b^2 = c^2
   \]
   Substituting the known values:
   \[
   3^2 + b^2 = 5^2 \\
   9 + b^2 = 25 \\
   b^2 = 16 \\
   b = 4 \, \text{meters}
   \]

3. **Calculate the area of the right triangle:**
   \[
   \text{Area} = \frac{\text{base} \times \text{height}}{2} = \frac{3 \times 4}{2} = 6 \, \text{square meters}
   \]

**Answer:** The area of the triangle is 6 square meters.
