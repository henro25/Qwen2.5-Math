Answer: Given that \(a\) and \(b\) are the roots of the equation \(x^2 - mx + 2 = 0\), we know from vieta's formulas that:
\[ a + b = m \]
\[ ab = 2 \]

we need to find \(q\) for the quadratic equation \(x^2 - px + q = 0\) whose roots are \(a + \frac{1}{b}\) and \(b + \frac{1}{a}\).

first, we calculate the product of the new roots:
\[
\left(a + \frac{1}{b}\right) \left(b + \frac{1}{a}\right)
\]

expanding this product:
\[
\left(a + \frac{1}{b}\right) \left(b + \frac{1}{a}\right) = ab + a \cdot \frac{1}{a} + \frac{1}{b} \cdot b + \frac{1}{b} \cdot \frac{1}{a}
\]
\[
= ab + 1 + 1 + \frac{1}{ab}
\]

substituting \(ab = 2\):
\[
= 2 + 1 + 1 + \frac{1}{2} = 4 + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{9}{2}
\]

thus, the value of \(q\) is:
\[
\boxed{\frac{9}{2}}
\]
