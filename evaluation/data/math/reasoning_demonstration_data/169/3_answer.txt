Answer: To find the area of the parallelogram, we use the formula:

\[ \text{area} = \text{base} \times \text{height} \]

given the base is 15 feet and the height is 3 feet, we calculate:

\[ 15 \, \text{ft} \times 3 \, \text{ft} = 45 \, \text{square feet} \]

to verify, we use the shoelace formula with the vertices (0,0), (15,0), (19,3), and (4,3):

\[ \text{area} = \frac{1}{2} \left| 0 \cdot 0 + 15 \cdot 3 + 19 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot 0 - (0 \cdot 15 + 0 \cdot 19 + 3 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot 0) \right| \]
\[ = \frac{1}{2} \left| 0 + 45 + 57 + 0 - (0 + 0 + 12 + 0) \right| \]
\[ = \frac{1}{2} \left| 102 - 12 \right| \]
\[ = \frac{1}{2} \times 90 = 45 \, \text{square feet} \]

alternatively, using vectors for sides (15,0) and (4,3):

\[ \text{area} = |15 \times 3 - 0 \times 4| = |45| = 45 \, \text{square feet} \]

thus, the area of the parallelogram is:

\boxed{45}
