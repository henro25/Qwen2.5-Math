\[
\mathbf{a} \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -15 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix} = -3 \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}
\]

this confirms that \(\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}\) is an eigenvector of \(\mathbf{a}\) with eigenvalue \(-3\).

using the property of eigenvalues, we have:

\[
\mathbf{a}^5 \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = (-3)^5 \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}
\]

calculating \((-3)^5\):

\[
(-3)^5 = -243
\]

thus:

\[
\mathbf{a}^5 \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = -243 \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -243 \cdot 5 \\ -243 \cdot (-2) \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -1215 \\ 486 \end{pmatrix}
\]

therefore, the final answer is:
