Answer: To calculate \(\frac{3 \cdot 5! + 15 \cdot 4!}{6!}\), we start by computing the factorials:

\[
5! = 120, \quad 4! = 24, \quad 6! = 720
\]

substitute these values into the expression:

\[
\frac{3 \cdot 120 + 15 \cdot 24}{720}
\]

calculate the products:

\[
3 \cdot 120 = 360, \quad 15 \cdot 24 = 360
\]

add the results:

\[
360 + 360 = 720
\]

divide by \(6!\):

\[
\frac{720}{720} = 1
\]

alternatively, we can factor out \(4!\) from the numerator:

\[
\frac{3 \cdot 5! + 15 \cdot 4!}{6!} = \frac{4! \cdot (3 \cdot 5 + 15)}{6!} = \frac{4! \cdot 30}{6!}
\]

since \(6! = 6 \cdot 5 \cdot 4!\):

\[
\frac{4! \cdot 30}{6 \cdot 5 \cdot 4!} = \frac{30}{30} = 1
\]

thus, the final answer is:

\[
\boxed{1}
\]
