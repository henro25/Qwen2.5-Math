Answer: To simplify \((3 - 2i)^2\), we use the binomial expansion formula \((a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2\), where \(a = 3\) and \(b = 2i\).

first, we compute each part step by step:

1. \((3)^2 = 9\)
2. \(2 \times 3 \times 2i = 12i\)
3. \((2i)^2 = 4i^2 = 4 \times (-1) = -4\)

combining these results:

\[
(3 - 2i)^2 = 9 - 12i - 4
\]

combining the real parts:

\[
9 - 4 = 5
\]

thus, the expression simplifies to:

\[
5 - 12i
\]

to confirm, we use the foil method:

\[
(3 - 2i)(3 - 2i) = 3 \times 3 + 3 \times (-2i) + (-2i) \times 3 + (-2i) \times (-2i)
\]

calculating each term:

1. \(3 \times 3 = 9\)
2. \(3 \times (-2i) = -6i\)
3. \(-2i \times 3 = -6i\)
4. \(-2i \times -2i = 4i^2 = 4 \times (-1) = -4\)

adding them together:

\[
9 - 6i - 6i - 4 = 9 - 12i - 4 = 5 - 12i
\]

both methods confirm that the simplified form is:

\[
\boxed{5 - 12i}
\]
