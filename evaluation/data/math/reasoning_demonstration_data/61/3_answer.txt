Answer: First, we find the semi-perimeter \( s \) of triangle \( abc \):

\[
s = \frac{ab + bc + ac}{2} = \frac{13 + 14 + 15}{2} = 21
\]

next, we calculate the area \( a \) of the triangle using heron's formula:

\[
a = \sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)} = \sqrt{21(21-13)(21-14)(21-15)} = \sqrt{21 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6} = \sqrt{7056} = 84
\]

the inradius \( r \) is given by:

\[
r = \frac{a}{s} = \frac{84}{21} = 4
\]

to find the length of \( bi \), we use the formula:

\[
bi = \sqrt{r^2 + (s - b)^2}
\]

where \( b = ac = 15 \), \( s = 21 \), and \( r = 4 \):

\[
bi = \sqrt{4^2 + (21 - 15)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 36} = \sqrt{52} = 2\sqrt{13}
\]

thus, the length of \( bi \) is:

\[
\boxed{2\sqrt{13}}
\]
