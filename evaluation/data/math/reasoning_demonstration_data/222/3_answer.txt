Answer: To find the smallest positive angle \(\theta\) for which

\[
\cos \theta = \sin 60^\circ + \cos 42^\circ - \sin 12^\circ - \cos 6^\circ,
\]

we start by using known trigonometric values and identities to simplify the right-hand side.

first, we use the difference of sines and cosines identities:

\[
\sin 60^\circ - \sin 12^\circ = 2 \cos 36^\circ \sin 24^\circ,
\]
\[
\cos 42^\circ - \cos 6^\circ = -2 \sin 24^\circ \sin 18^\circ.
\]

combining these, we get:

\[
\sin 60^\circ - \sin 12^\circ + \cos 42^\circ - \cos 6^\circ = 2 \cos 36^\circ \sin 24^\circ - 2 \sin 24^\circ \sin 18^\circ.
\]

factoring out \(2 \sin 24^\circ\):

\[
2 \sin 24^\circ (\cos 36^\circ - \sin 18^\circ).
\]

using the identity \(\cos 36^\circ = \sin 54^\circ\), we simplify further:

\[
\cos 36^\circ - \sin 18^\circ = \sin 54^\circ - \sin 18^\circ = 2 \cos 36^\circ \sin 18^\circ.
\]

thus, the expression becomes:

\[
2 \sin 24^\circ (2 \cos 36^\circ \sin 18^\circ) = 4 \sin 24^\circ \cos 36^\circ \sin 18^\circ.
\]

next, we calculate the numerical values of the trigonometric functions:

\[
\sin 60^\circ \approx 0.8660, \quad \cos 42^\circ \approx 0.7431, \quad \sin 12^\circ \approx 0.2079, \quad \cos 6^\circ \approx 0.9945.
\]

summing these values:

\[
0.8660 + 0.7431 - 0.2079 - 0.9945 \approx 0.4067.
\]

thus, we have:

\[
\cos \theta \approx 0.4067.
\]

using a calculator to find \(\theta\):

\[
\theta \approx \cos^{-1}(0.4067) \approx 66^\circ.
\]

therefore, the smallest positive angle \(\theta\) is:

\[
\boxed{66^\circ}
\]
